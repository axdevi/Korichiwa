Syllable Block Formula
Korean syllables always follow: Consonant + Vowel (+ optional final consonant) Example: 한 = ㅎ + ㅏ + ㄴ | 글 = ㄱ + ㅡ + ㄹ | 어 = ㅇ + ㅓ
Essential Pronunciation Rules
  • Liaison (연음): Final consonant moves to next syllable's initial — 먹어요 → [머거요]
  • Nasalization: ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ + ㄴ/ㅁ → become nasal — 국물 → [궁물]
  • Aspiration: ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅈ + ㅎ → aspirated — 좋다 → [조타]
  • Tensification: After unreleased stops, plain consonants become tense — 학교 → [학꾜]

Honorific Speech (존댓말)
  • Subject honorific: -으시/시- inserted in stem → 가다 → 가세요 | 드시다 (eat, polite)
  • Object honorific: 드리다 (give to senior) vs 주다 (give generally)
  • Special honorific vocab: 밥→진지, 집→댁, 이름→성함, 나이→연세, 아프다→편찮다
  • Formal polite: -습니다/ㅂ니다 (presentations, strangers) vs -해요 (everyday polite)

🎯 TOPIK I Level 2 — Exam Guide
Test Format
SectionQuestionsTimePoints
Listening (듣기)30 questions40 min100 pts
Reading (읽기)40 questions60 min100 pts
Total70 questions100 min200 pts
Level 2 pass score——80–139 pts
Listening Question Types
  • Q1–4: Short dialogues — choose the matching picture or next response
  • Q5–8: Short conversations — choose what topic/situation it is
  • Q9–12: Medium dialogues — choose the central topic or correct inference
  • Q13–16: Longer monologues — listen twice, answer comprehension questions
  • 💡 Key tip: Focus on first and last sentences — they carry the main idea
Reading Question Types
  • Q1–8: Vocabulary — choose meaning, fill in the blank
  • Q9–18: Short texts (signs, ads, notices) — choose correct info
  • Q19–24: Medium texts — choose main idea or fill missing sentence
  • Q25–30: Arrange sentences / find the odd one out
  • Q31–40: Longer passages — inference and comprehension
